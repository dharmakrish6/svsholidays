Why Rajasthan

The most-visited state in India — and still the most underexplored.

Everyone knows the Golden Triangle. We avoid it. Not because Jaipur, Agra and Delhi aren't worth visiting — they are — but because Rajasthan's real depth lies elsewhere: in the painted havelis of Shekhawati, in a hill fort at five in the morning before the buses arrive, in a village stay with a family whose ancestors were court musicians.

We design Rajasthan itineraries that are anchored in the famous and enriched by the hidden. The Amber Fort is extraordinary. So is the hour before it opens.

Sample Route · 10 Nights

A Rajasthan we designed for one family last winter

This is one version of Rajasthan. Your version will be different. But this gives you a sense of how we think about pacing, what we prioritise and what we deliberately leave out.

Day 1 Arrival in Jaipur · The Pink City at its own pace +

Arrive into Jaipur. No fort visits today — on day one, we let the city wash over you. Afternoon walk through the old city bazaars with a local textile historian (not a guide, a practitioner). Dinner at a restored haveli rooftop.

The itinerary begins gently by design. Jetlag and Rajasthan don't mix well when you're staring at ramparts wondering what time it is.

Stay:  Samode Haveli, Jaipur · Heritage guesthouse, family-owned since 1784
Day 2 Amber Fort · Early, before the crowds, with a historian +

We arrive at Amber at 6:45am when the gate opens. By 10am, it belongs to everyone else. You'll have had three and a half hours in the Sheesh Mahal — the Hall of Mirrors — and the zenana quarters with a historian who has been studying this fort for twenty years.

Afternoon: Jaipuri block printing workshop. Not a demonstration. You'll print something.

Stay:  Samode Haveli, Jaipur
Days 3–4 Shekhawati · The open-air fresco gallery nobody talks about +

Shekhawati is the answer to the question: what does Rajasthan look like away from the palace circuit? Merchant families who made their fortunes in Calcutta during the Raj returned home and commissioned havelis decorated floor-to-ceiling in frescoes — scenes from Hindu mythology jostling with steam engines, cars, and portraits of the King Emperor.

We spend two days here with a local historian who grew up in the region. Many of the havelis are privately owned and not officially open. She knows which families will show you inside over a cup of chai.

Stay:  Castle Mandawa · 16th-century fort converted by the family themselves
Days 5–6 Jodhpur · The Blue City and the Mehrangarh +

Mehrangarh Fort is, in our view, the most extraordinary fort in India — and the finest museum. We spend half a day inside with a curator. The other half is given to the old city: a cooking class in a home kitchen in the blue lanes, watching the light shift on the houses as the afternoon deepens.

Day 6 is flexible by design. We often add a dawn hot-air balloon over the fort for those who want it, or a day trip to Bishnoi villages to meet the community whose environmental ethic predates the modern environmental movement by four centuries.

Stay:  Raas Jodhpur · Boutique property directly below the fort walls
Days 7–8 The Thar Desert · A night under no artificial light +

We don't take clients to Jaisalmer's main dune — the one with the camel rides and the DJ. We go to a private concession twenty minutes further that is genuinely empty. One camp. Eight tents. Complete silence from sunset onwards.

Day 8 is the return journey, stopping at Jaisalmer Fort (one of the world's only living forts — families have lived inside it for nine hundred years) and a late lunch in the shadow of its walls.

Night 7:  Private desert camp, Thar · Nights 8: Suryagarh, Jaisalmer
Days 9–10 Udaipur · The lake city as an ending, not a beginning +

Most Rajasthan itineraries start in Udaipur. We end there, because it's the kind of city that rewards slowing down — and by day nine, you're ready to slow down. Lake Pichola at dusk from the right rooftop. The City Palace museum. A boat to the Jagmandir at golden hour.

Day 10: morning at leisure before departure transfers.

Stay:  Taj Lake Palace or Raas Devigarh (your briefing decides which)

Where We Stay

Stays we'd recommend without hesitation

We don't have contractual obligations with any property. These are stays we've vetted ourselves.

Heritage · Jaipur

Samode Haveli

A family-owned haveli in the old city, run by the same family for over two centuries. Rooms built around inner courtyards still lit by oil lamps at dinner.

Boutique · Jodhpur

Raas Jodhpur

Contemporary design directly below the Mehrangarh walls. The rooftop pool looks straight into the fort. One of the better architectural decisions in Indian hospitality.

Desert Camp · Jaisalmer

Private Thar Camp

Our preferred private concession. Eight tents, no generator after 10pm, a chef who cooks from a wood fire, and a sky without a single visible horizon light.
