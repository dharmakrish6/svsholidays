How We Travel

Journeys shaped around who you are, not where we send everyone.

Six experience types. Infinite variations. Every one built from scratch for the person making the enquiry.

The Approach

We start with a question, not an itinerary.

When you reach out to us, the first thing we ask isn't "how many days?" or "what's your budget?" It's something closer to: what do you want this trip to feel like?

That question shapes everything — who we pair you with on the ground, which property sits at the centre of each night, whether we book you a private transfer or a sleeper train.

I

Honeymoons

We understand that a honeymoon isn't a holiday — it's a statement. It needs to feel effortless, private and unlike anything either of you has experienced before.

Our honeymoon itineraries run from five nights to three weeks. We match the pace, the region and the properties to how you actually want to spend the time — not what photographs best on social media.

Popular pairings: Kerala + Maldives · Rajasthan + Ranthambore · Goa + Andaman Islands

Private properties Couples dining Water & coast Heritage palaces
II

Family Journeys

We travel with our own families. We know that the window between "Are we there yet?" and "Actually, this is incredible" is narrower than it looks — and we know how to stay in it.

We design family trips across three generations, from a grandparent who needs lift access to a teenager who needs wifi at dinner. We account for both, without making either feel like a compromise.

Multi-generation Activity mix School-holiday timing Accessible properties
III

Heritage & Royal India

The palace hotels everyone knows about aren't the point. We go deeper — a 400-year-old haveli with four rooms and a family who has lived in it for fifteen generations; a private evening in a fort that closes to tourists at five.

Our heritage itineraries are built around access: to spaces, to knowledge, to the kind of evening that doesn't appear on any booking platform.

Private access Expert historians Heritage stays Curated dinners
IV

Wild India

We work exclusively with naturalists and conservationists — not generic guides — so that what you understand about what you're seeing is as extraordinary as seeing it.

Ranthambore, Kanha, Bandhavgarh, Satpura, Tadoba. We know which zones, which jeeps, which camps sit close enough to feel immersed without the generator noise.

Tiger reserves Expert naturalists Luxury camps Bird specialists
V

Himalayan Expeditions

The Himalaya is not a backdrop. It takes time to understand — altitude, acclimatisation, weather windows — and we build in all of it, quietly, so you never feel managed.

Ladakh's high passes. Sikkim's Dzongu valley. The Zanskar in winter. We design Himalayan journeys that respect the mountain's pace and reward it with the kind of access that rushed trips never reach.

Altitude planning Monastery permits Remote camps Trek + culture mix
VI

Solo & Small Group

Solo travel in India is extraordinary and underrated. We make it safe, seamless and social — not in a group-tour way, but in the sense that you'll share a table or a jeep with the right person at the right moment.

For curated small groups: we cap at six. We choose the travellers as carefully as we choose the itinerary. The dinner conversation is part of the design.

Solo travellers Groups max 6 Curated matchmaking Flexible pacing
